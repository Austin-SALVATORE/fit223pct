# Fitness Knowledge Pack

## Purpose

This document is a long-term reference for development decisions, not a
workout program.

## Principles

-   Prioritize consistency over intensity.
-   Train each major muscle group about twice per week.
-   Progressive overload may come from load, reps, sets, tempo, range of
    motion, technique, or unilateral work.
-   Leave 1--3 repetitions in reserve (RIR) on most working sets.
-   Avoid unnecessary failure on technical compound lifts.
-   Recovery is part of training.

## Austin's Equipment

-   Adjustable barbell (0--30 kg)
-   Separate dumbbells (1×20 kg or 2×10 kg)
-   Adjustable bench
-   Resistance bands

Design training around equipment limitations instead of pretending
commercial gym access.

## Coaching Style

Explain WHY before WHAT. Teach one concept at the right moment. Never
shame missed workouts.

## Recovery

Track: - Sleep - Energy - Soreness - Stress - Motivation

Adjust training volume when appropriate rather than forcing adherence.

## Progression

Use double progression by default. When load cannot increase: - Increase
reps - Slow eccentric - Pause - Improve ROM - Increase stability - Use
unilateral versions

## Success Metrics

Primary: - Consistency - Waist trend - Strength trend Secondary: - Scale
weight - Session completion
