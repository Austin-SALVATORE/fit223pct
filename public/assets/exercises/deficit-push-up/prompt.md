# Deficit Push-up — image generation prompt

> The differentiator from a standard push-up is the deficit: each hand is on a
> low riser, so at the bottom the chest descends BELOW the level of the hands.
> The extra depth at the bottom frame is the whole point of this asset.

## Prompt

```text
Create one wide instructional fitness illustration: a single horizontal strip
showing the SAME person performing one repetition, read left to right.

MOVEMENT FOR THIS IMAGE:
Exercise: Deficit Push-up

- Hands stay flat on the riser tops, wrists neutral, fingers spread; the
  risers never tip or slide.
- Head stays neutral and in line with the spine, chin not craned forward.
- Chest and hips rise and fall at the same rate.
```

- [ ] Elbows tucked to roughly 45 degrees, never flared to 90
- [ ] Both risers identical size and position in every frame
- [ ] Hands flat on the riser tops with neutral wrists
- [ ] Same face, hair, wardrobe, and body proportions in all six frames
- [ ] All six figures identical scale on one shared ground line
- [ ] Readable as a deficit push-up (not a floor push-up) at 64 px wide
